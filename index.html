About This Dive

Superman's Flight is the crown jewel of Soufrière's drift diving. Named for the sensation of flying as the current carries you along a dramatic underwater wall, this site delivers an exhilarating experience that keeps divers coming back year after year.

The dive begins at the southern edge of the marine protected area, where the bottom quickly drops from 12m to beyond 30m along a steep coral-covered wall. The current typically flows northward, allowing divers to glide effortlessly over gardens of vibrant tube sponges, sea fans, and massive barrel sponges while the wall drops away into the deep blue.

What makes Superman's Flight truly special is the consistency of marine life encounters. Horse-eye jacks swarm in glittering clouds, sea turtles graze on sponges, and spotted eagle rays cruise past at the edge of visibility. On exceptional days, you might spot a reef shark patrolling the deeper waters beyond the wall.

This is the site that inspired the name Action Adventure Divers — it's the dive that makes everyone feel like a superhero. Suitable for intermediate divers and above, the experience is both thrilling and safe under the guidance of our experienced team.

About This Dive

Malgretoute is the quintessential St. Lucia wall dive — accessible, beautiful, and teeming with life. Its gentle slope makes it one of the most versatile sites in Soufrière, welcoming beginners while offering plenty for experienced divers willing to go deeper.

The reef starts in just 5m of water, making it ideal for Discover Scuba participants and those easing back into diving. As you descend along the sandy slope, the coral cover becomes denser, transitioning from scattered boulder coral to a magnificent wall face draped in soft corals, rope sponges, and dense sea fans at around 18m.

Malgretoute is particularly famous for its macro life. Patient photographers can find seahorses clinging to sea fans, frogfish camouflaged among sponges, and octopus peeking from crevices. The site is also a reliable spot for turtle encounters — several green sea turtles have been resident here for years.

With calm conditions most of the year and excellent visibility, Malgretoute is often our first-choice site for check-out dives, refresher courses, and anyone wanting a relaxed, visually stunning introduction to St. Lucia's underwater world.

About This Dive

The Pinnacles is a truly dramatic dive site featuring three ancient volcanic spires that rise from the sandy sea floor like underwater cathedrals. This advanced site rewards experienced divers with some of the most exciting topography in Soufrière Bay.

The pinnacles start at around 15m and descend to over 30m, with the main spires surrounded by boulder fields and sandy channels. Each pinnacle is densely covered in marine growth — orange cup corals, rope sponges, and black coral trees cling to the steep faces, while clouds of small reef fish swirl around the summits.

This site is famous for larger pelagics. Barracuda hang in the water column at the edge of visibility, horse-eye jacks form massive bait balls during the hunting season, and nurse sharks are frequently found resting in the sandy gullies between the pinnacles. The swim-throughs at the base of the spires add an element of exploration.

The Pinnacles require good buoyancy control due to the variable depths and swim-throughs. The topography creates localized currents that can shift during the dive, making this a site best suited for Advanced Open Water divers and above. The rewards are unmatched.

About This Dive

Fairlyland lives up to its name — a fairy-tale reef garden of shallow corals bursting with colour and life. As one of the most accessible sites in Soufrière Bay, it's a favourite for beginner divers, snorkellers, night dives, and underwater photographers.

The site is a protected cove with a sandy bottom scattered with bommies — isolated coral heads that have grown into vibrant oases of marine activity. Elkhorn coral, brain coral, and delicate sea fans create a three-dimensional maze that shelters an astonishing variety of marine life.

Night diving at Fairlyland is particularly magical. As darkness falls, the daytime inhabitants retreat and nocturnal creatures emerge — octopus hunting across the sand, slipper lobsters crawling from crevices, and basket stars unfurling their intricate arms to filter-feed in the current. It's a completely different world after sunset.

With a maximum depth of just 18m, Fairlyland offers long bottom times and stress-free diving. The sandy patches between coral heads are perfect for practicing skills, making this a favourite for Open Water course students and refresher dives.

About This Dive

Chester's Fave isn't just a clever name — it truly is the personal favourite of our founder Chester Nathaniel. After thousands of dives across Soufrière Bay, this is the site he chooses when he wants to remember why he fell in love with the ocean.

The site combines the best elements of a wall dive and a gentle drift. The reef slopes from 10m to an abrupt drop-off at around 18m, then continues as a vertical wall to 28m+. The current runs predictably along the wall, giving divers a controlled drift past some of the healthiest coral formations in the bay.

Chester's Fave is renowned for its marine life density. On any given dive, you might encounter multiple sea turtles feeding on the sponges, large schools of jacks hunting in the blue, and eagle rays gliding along the wall edge. The coral here is exceptionally healthy, with massive brain corals and colourful tube sponges covering every surface.

The best time to dive here is during the dry season (December to May) when visibility regularly exceeds 25m. Chester himself leads this dive whenever possible, sharing his intimate knowledge of the site's nooks, crannies, and regular residents.

About This Dive

Captain Chili's pick — a deeper drift dive along an exhilarating wall that pushes into the big blue. This is the site for experienced divers who want to feel the thrill of the open ocean while remaining absolutely safe under expert guidance.

The dive profile is straightforward but demanding: drop to 18m on the reef crest, then follow the wall as it descends into the depths. The current here is typically stronger than other sites, creating the perfect conditions for larger pelagics to patrol the wall edge. Chili knows exactly where the current will be on any given day.

Chili's Fave offers the best chance of encountering eagle rays in Soufrière. They cruise the wall edge in small groups, often passing close enough for an unforgettable encounter. Nurse sharks rest in sandy alcoves along the wall, and on rare occasions, larger shark species have been sighted in the blue beyond.

Due to the depth and current, this site is restricted to Advanced Open Water divers with recent diving experience. The best conditions occur from January through April when the trade winds are steady and visibility is at its peak.

About This Dive

Anse Chastanet Reef is the perfect example of why you don't need a boat to experience world-class diving in St. Lucia. This beautiful fringing reef starts just metres from the shore at Anse Chastanet, making it one of the most accessible dive sites in the Caribbean.

The reef fringes the entire bay, with hard coral formations beginning in just 3m of water and extending outward to a max depth of about 16m. The shallow profile means long, relaxed dives with abundant natural light that brings out the vibrant colours of the coral — brain coral, sheet coral, and massive stands of elkhorn coral.

Anse Chastanet is particularly good for macro photography. The reef is home to numerous species of small colourful marine life including flamingo tongues on the sea fans, arrow crabs hiding in the coral heads, and the occasional seahorse clinging to soft coral. Sandy patches between coral formations are excellent hunting grounds for peacock flounder and southern stingrays.

This site is ideal for Discover Scuba sessions, refresher dives, and snorkellers. The easy access means we can dive here even when conditions are less favourable for boat dives, making it a reliable year-round option for all skill levels.

About This Dive

Trou Diable ("Devil's Hole") is the most challenging dive site in Soufrière — and for experienced divers seeking the ultimate thrill, the most rewarding. This deep drift dive takes you into the heart of the channel between St. Lucia and Martinique, where oceanic conditions create a world-class adrenaline dive.

The site features a deep channel cut into the ancient volcanic rock, dropping from 25m to over 40m. The walls of the channel are draped in deep-water gorgonians and black coral, home to marine life rarely seen on shallower reefs. The current here is consistent and can be strong, making this strictly an advanced-only site.

Trou Diable offers the best chance of seeing larger marine life. Reef sharks are regular visitors to the channel, drawn by the abundant fish life. Spotted eagle rays cruise the depths in small schools, and massive horse-eye jacks form tornado-like bait balls during the hunting season. The deep blue visibility regularly exceeds 30m on good days.

This dive requires careful planning. Due to the depth and potential for strong currents, we limit bottom times and ensure each diver has deep-diving experience. The rewards are commensurate with the challenge — Trou Diable is the dive that advanced divers dream about long after they've returned home.

Stewardship

Protecting What We Love

The ocean gives us everything. We believe it's our responsibility to give back. Here's how we're working to protect St. Lucia's marine ecosystems for future generations.

Reef Restoration

We participate in local coral restoration projects, helping to propagate and transplant resilient coral species onto degraded reef areas. Our team monitors restoration sites and reports on growth and survival rates to Soufrière Marine Management Area.

Lionfish Management

Chester is an active participant in the lionfish management program, regularly spearing invasive lionfish during dives to protect native species. We educate our divers about the lionfish threat and encourage responsible removal.

Reef-Safe Sunscreen

We require all guests to use reef-safe sunscreen. Chemical sunscreens containing oxybenzone and octinoxate damage coral larvae and contribute to bleaching. We provide reef-safe options at the shop.

No-Touch Diving

We teach and enforce a strict no-touch diving policy. Coral polyps are fragile — even a gentle touch can damage them. We brief every diver on proper buoyancy control to ensure minimal impact on the reef.

Community Partnerships

We work with local schools, the Soufrière Marine Management Association, and regional conservation organizations. Our team participates in beach clean-ups, reef monitoring, and ocean literacy programs.

How You Can Help

Every diver who chooses Action Adventure Divers supports local conservation. You can also contribute directly to the Soufrière Marine Protected Area through donations or by participating in our eco-dive programs. Ask us how
